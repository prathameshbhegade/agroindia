There are pests like shoot fly,aphid,stem borer,midge fly which cn be controlled by following techniques

Nature of damage/infestation:
1. The larva migrates to the upper side of the leaf, and moves along the leaf whorl until it reaches the growing point where the larvae cut the growing point.
2. As a result the central leaf dries up forming a dead heart, which can be pulled out easily and produces a rotting smell.
3. Normally the damage occurs 1 to 4 weeks after seedling emergence and will lead to the typical dead heart symptoms.
4. Seedlings of 5 to 30 days old are generally susceptible to shoot fly damage.
5. Older plants (>30 days after seedling emergence) are not usually damaged by shoot fly however, under conditions of high humidity during the rainy season, infestation may occur.
Biological Control:
NSKE 5% @ 500 ml/ 10 L of water.

Nature of damage:
1. Nymphs and Adults suck the cell sap from leaves and plant parts.
2. Incidence is severe at the crop growing stages.
3. While feeding they secrete the honey dew substances from the body, on which black sooty moulds develop, which reduces photosynthesis activity. Crop production ultimately decreases.

Nature of damage:
1. Maggot (Carve) feed on the developing ovary inside the glumes: this results in empty or chaffy spikelets as shrivelled grains fail to develop.
2. During the grain filling or milk stage, if damaged spikelets are pressed between the finger and thumb or between a pair of forceps, they produce a red ooze: the body contents of the midge larva or pupa.
3. In the mature crop, damaged spikelets become empty or chaffy, and the sorghum panicles present a blasted appearance.
4. Damaged panicles have small, transparent midge pupal cases attached to the tip of the damaged spikelets
