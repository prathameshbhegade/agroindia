Chilli is grown in many types of soils -sandy to heavy clay. pH of 6-7 are best suited for cultivation. As a rain-fed crop it is grown in areas receiving an annual rainfall of 75-100cm. Maximum temperature ranging from 20-30°C and a minimum temperature not below 10°C is ideal.

Provide adequate and proper irrigation by knowing soil moisture. Drip irrigation is also the effective method where use of Drip Laterals:12mm or 16 mm and Drippers : 4 LPH is recommended.

Before transplanting Total requirement of N, P and K is 40:20:20 kg/acre, 50 % of total NPK is applied as basal dose and remaining is given in splitted doses as per crop growth stages.

Thrips and Aphids are major sucking pest infesting Chilli crops. These insects suck sap from leaves. Affected leaves curl upwards along the margin and get crinkled and reduced in size. Spray NSKE 5% or Neem oil, in case of heavy infestation take one spray of Spinosad 45 % SC or Emamectin benzoate 5 % SG as per recommendation.
