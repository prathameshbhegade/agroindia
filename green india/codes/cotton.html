At the time of sowing Cotton crop requires total 50 kg N, 24 kg P and 24 kg K per acre. Nitrogen is applied in three split doses i.e. 10 kg N and remaining 40 kg in two equal split doses after 30 and 60 days after sowing. A light irrigation is given immediately after sowing

Aphids, Jassids, Thrips, Whiteflies and Mealybugs are major sucking pests attacking on the cotton crop. Jassids damage leaves and give it a bronze or brick red colour which is typical “hopper burn” symptom. Due to sucking pest complex, leaves become wrinkled, curl upwards and curling and crinkling of leaves is also observed. For effective control, follow integrated pest management method. Avoid excessive nitrogen application. Install Yellow sticky traps. Spray Neem oil, Verticillium lecanii as a preventative measure. In case of heavy pest population spray chemicals like Acephate 75% or Flonicamid 50WG or Buprofezin 25 % SC. American Bollworm, Spotted bollworm and Pink Bollworm are major types of bollworms that attack the cotton crop. Timely management is required to protect the bolls from damage and crop losses. Install Bird perches/ Use pheromone traps. Regular application of Neem oil/ Beauveria bassiana/ Bacillus thuringiensis is useful. In case of more bollworm damage, use any one of the following insecticide: Emamectin benzoate 5 SG or Profenophos 50 EC or Thiodicarb 75 WP as per recommended dose
