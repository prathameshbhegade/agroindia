The optimum pH range is 6.0 - 7.5. The best soil for successful onion cultivation is deep, friable loam and alluvial soils with good drainage. The optimum temperature is 13-25˚C. It requires about 70% relative humidity with the average annual rainfall 650-750 mm.

Onion needs frequent light irrigations to maintain optimum soil moisture. Give light irrigation depending on rainfall and water stress situation.

At the time of planting Fertilizer requirement of onion crop is NPKS 40:20:20:12 respectively. Full dose of P, K and S and 50% dose of N at the time of transplanting, remaining 50% of nitrogen should be applied in split doses.

Thrips suck the cell sap from leaves which appear as shining silver streaks or have brown spots. Leaves damaged by mites do not open completely. Whole plant shows stunting, twisting, curling and yellow mottling symptoms. Spray Neem oil/ Beauveria bassiana to control pest losses on regular basis. In case of mite damage, spray Dicofol @ 20gm/ 10L .

90 days after transplanting When the bulbs developing from the leaf bases of onions are fully formed, the leafy green tops begin to turn yellow and eventually collapse at a point a little above the top of the bulb, leaving an upright short neck. Harvesting at proper stage increases the storage life of onion crop. Average yield varies from 10-12 t/ acre.
